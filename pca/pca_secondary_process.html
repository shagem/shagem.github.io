Genome Sequencing and Cloning Process

*Please note, exact procedures and outcomes may vary from patient to patient*

Step 1: Obtaining and preparing the sample

The first step in every service or procedure we offer begins with a mail-in home DNA sample kit. The patient is required to provide a cheek swab or sample bottle of saliva using the provided materials, and simply mail the kit back to the posted address.

Once the sample has reached the PCA testing facility it is seperated and placed in isolation to avoid contamination. PCA scientist then prepare each sample individually using a process called polymerase chain reaction. This preperation is fundamental in the procedure as it allows our scientist to make multiple copies of a patients DNA for testing.

The remaining original sample is then kept as reference and stored in a deep freeze vault at a secure PCA facility while it's copies begin the initial testing.

Step 2: Harvesting and testing the genetic material

The DNA sample is put through a process known as capillary sequencing in which DNA is broken up randomly into numerous small segments, which are sequenced using the chain termination method to obtain reads. Multiple overlapping reads for the target DNA are obtained by performing several rounds of this fragmentation and sequencing. Advanced computer programs then use the overlapping ends of different reads to assemble them into a continuous sequence.
